A long weekend in Romania

Bucharest & the Carpathians

City buzz for a night, then mountains, castles and medieval Saxon villages an hour up the road. Everything here is within easy reach for a Friday-to-Sunday.

Fri arrival → Sun departure

The short version

Bucharest is hot and lively in summer, but the magic is north. 90 minutes out of the city you hit the Carpathians: real mountains, lakes, royal castles, and a Dracula legend you can lean into.

Best play for the weekend: one easy evening in Bucharest, then get her out of the city and into the mountains. Castles, a cable car onto an alpine plateau, dinner in a 800-year-old square, and a fortified Saxon village that looks frozen in the Middle Ages. That combination does the work for you.

Practical: rent a car and drive yourself. It's an easy run up the DN1 / A3 and having your own wheels makes the castle-hopping and village detours effortless. Roads get busy on summer weekends, so leave early. Grab the rental at the airport when she lands.

Day 1 — Bucharest

She lands Friday. Keep it easy and atmospheric, no big drives.

Old Town (Lipscani) for the evening

The historic core: cobbled streets, terraces, wine bars, live music spilling out of everywhere. Easy to wander, easy to charm.

  • Caru' cu Bere — the classic. A jaw-dropping neo-gothic beer hall from 1899, stained glass and carved wood, proper Romanian food. Book ahead.
  • Cărturești Carusel — a gorgeous white spiral bookstore, great for a daytime photo moment.
  • Late drinks at a rooftop or one of the speakeasies tucked off Lipscani.

If you have daylight

  • Romanian Athenaeum — stunning concert hall
  • Palace of Parliament — the absurdly huge one
  • Village Museum — open-air, by the lake in Herăstrău Park

Wellness card to play

Therme Bucharest, just outside the city, is one of Europe's largest wellness complexes. Pools, palm trees, saunas. Given she's in the longevity/health world, it's a perfect relaxed afternoon and very on-brand for you.

Day 2 — Into the mountains

The big day. Drive up the Prahova Valley, hit the royal castle, then Dracula's castle, and land in Brașov for the night.

Stop 1 · ~1h45 from Bucharest

Sinaia — Peleș Castle

Start here. Peleș is the showstopper: a fairytale Neo-Renaissance royal palace tucked into the forest, all turrets and carved wood. Genuinely one of the most beautiful castles in Europe and almost nobody outside Romania knows it. The Sinaia Monastery and the mountain town itself are worth a short wander too.

Book tickets aheadAllow 1.5–2h
Stop 2 · ~45 min onward

Bran Castle — "Dracula's Castle"

The Dracula hook. A dramatic clifftop castle that's become the Bram Stoker pilgrimage site. It's touristy but undeniably cool, and you get to do the whole "my friend says I'm descended from the line" bit. Great photos, fun energy.

Optional but iconic
Stop 3 · sleep here

Brașov — overnight

One of the most beautiful medieval towns in Europe and the perfect base. Pastel old town, the Black Church, the lively Council Square ringed with restaurants, and the Tâmpa cable car for a sunset view over the red rooftops. Have dinner in the square. This is the romantic anchor of the trip.

Stay 1 nightDinner in Council Square
Alternative for Day 2 if you'd rather hike: from Bușteni take the cable car up onto the Bucegi plateau — alpine meadows at 2,000m with the famous Sphinx and Babele rock formations. Easy walking, huge views, no serious effort required.

Day 3 — A Saxon village, then home

Sunday. Brașov in the morning, then the place that seals it before you head to the airport.

Viscri — the fortified Saxon village

This is the one Claudia and I went to. A tiny 12th-century Saxon village, UNESCO listed, with a white fortified church, dirt lanes, horse carts, and houses King Charles spent years restoring (he owns a house here). It feels like stepping 300 years back. Slow, beautiful, completely unlike anywhere else. Buy honey and wool from the locals, climb the church tower for the view over the hills.

~1h15 from BrașovUNESCO

If Viscri is too far given your flight, swap in Râșnov fortress (right by Bran) or the medieval citadel of Sighișoara — Dracula's actual birthplace, another UNESCO old town.

Logistics: Viscri → Bucharest airport is around 3.5h. If you fly Sunday evening it works; if it's a midday flight, do Râșnov or just a slow Brașov morning instead and drive straight back. Plan the day around your flight time and pad it for weekend traffic on the way down.

If you only remember five things

1 · Peleș Castle

The fairytale royal palace at Sinaia. Non-negotiable.

2 · Brașov for the night

Dinner in the medieval square. The romantic centre of the trip.

3 · Bran / Dracula

Lean into the legend. It's fun and she'll love the story.

4 · A Saxon village

Viscri or Sighișoara. The thing she won't have seen coming.

5 · Get out of the city

Bucharest for a night, mountains for the rest. That's the formula.

+ Rent a car
